Galeto

Description

Galeto is a dish that's not only a local favorite but also very popular with tourists visiting my city. I remember enjoying it a lot, especially at restaurants run by Italian colonists, those immigrant families who came to South Brazil years ago. These Italian-run spots served up galeto featuring young chicken marinated in a blend of herbs, garlic, and then grilled. It is often served with a homemade mayonnaise type of dish, which is almost completely different to the mayonnaise you may be thinking about, which is also used a lot in Churrascos (barbecues), the dish was also complemented by pasta with a type of sauce and polentas! An example of these can be seen with the galeto on the picture.

Ingredients

  • 6 kg of chicken
  • 1 handful of sage
  • 1/2 handful of marjoram
  • 1/2 bunch of parsley
  • 1 medium onion
  • 100 g of salt
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • Oregano to taste
  • White pepper
  • 100 ml of white wine
  • 120 ml of beer
  • 60 ml of olive oil

Preparation Method

  1. Chop the sage, marjoram, garlic, onion, and parsley.
  2. Mix the seasonings, adding salt, oregano, and pepper.
  3. Divide the chicken in half, remove the backbone, and cut it into pieces.
  4. In a container, layer the chicken with green seasonings, olive oil, and beer.
  5. Let it marinate for at least 12 hours.
  6. After marinating, brush the chicken with white wine, skewer it, and roast it over the coals for approximately 30 minutes.
